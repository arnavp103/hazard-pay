# Cold critique — round 1

The critic received only the five rendered screenshots, four captioned reference images, and the working Direction B language. It did not receive source, generator context, the plant manifest, or issue history.

## Verdict

**Ranking:** E > A > B > C > D

- **Passes:** none
- **Borderline:** E, A
- **Fails:** B, C, D

| Variant | Critic diagnosis | Smallest suggested regeneration |
| --- | --- | --- |
| E | Closest: selective magenta/chartreuse, overhead glyphs, and cable-like marks read as plausible cyberpunk technofantasy; still too blocky, flat, and clean. | Regenerate only the combatants with asymmetric silhouettes, dark contours, and cel-shaded clusters. |
| A | Strong value hierarchy and character-local accents coexist with the HUD, but figures are interchangeable blocks and the stage resembles UI geometry. | Replace rectangular anatomy with profession-driven silhouettes. |
| B | Coherent rust/cyan experiment, but too restrained, flat, and materially clean to own the central field. | Increase combatant contour/value contrast and add one local saturated accent per unit. |
| C | Adequate unit separation, but the global violet/blue wash flattens values and makes accents non-local. | Remap the environment to neutral dark massing and reserve violet/cyan for selective lights. |
| D | Compressed values dissolve contours, props, characters, and ground into one layer. | Restore a near-black contour/value tier before changing hue or detail. |

Confidence was high for palette, value, silhouette, material language, and still-frame readability. The critic could not judge animation readability, effect timing, hit clarity, or silhouette separation in motion.

## Discrimination result

Round 1 did **not** clear the proof bar. It found the planted palette and contour faults, but rejected both controls because all five shared a placeholder-anatomy confound. It also ranked the medieval-vocabulary plant first, interpreting its abstract glyphs as valid technofantasy. Round 2 therefore raises the shared silhouette/cel-shading floor while preserving the three planted variables.
