# Character and animation references for a comic-book cyberpunk direction

Research date: 2026-07-22

## Recommendation

Use two reference tiers and keep the boundary visible in the reference board:

1. **Vendorable:** openly licensed files that may be copied into the repository with
   the stated attribution and license metadata.
2. **Link-only:** copyrighted commercial work used for visual discussion. Link to the
   official page and preview, but do not download, crop, mirror, or commit its images.

The strongest center of gravity is **Huntdown** for cyberpunk setting and chunky
character readability, **MARVEL Cosmic Invasion** and **TMNT: Shredder's Revenge**
for comic-book exaggeration, and **ANNO: Mutationem** for detailed pixel characters
inside a cyberpunk world. **Gothicvania** and **Cyberpunk Portraits** are the most
useful open-license additions. The target is large enough for facial/material cues and
exaggerated key poses; it is not 16x16 icon art.

## Tier 1: open-license candidates safe to vendor

### 1. Cyberpunk Character Pack

- **Creator:** OcO
- **Source:** [creator's itch.io page](https://oco.itch.io/cyberpunk-character-pack)
- **Direct preview:** [creator-hosted animation GIF](https://img.itch.zone/aW1hZ2UvNjI5NDIyLzMzNTcyNzMuZ2lm/original/%2BlWwLm.gif)
- **License:** Custom permissive asset license. Exact source: the creator page's
  **Notes** allow modification and free/commercial project use while forbidding
  standalone resale; the page also prohibits generative-AI use.
- **What to learn:** 96x96 cells, bold contour masses, limited cel-like shading, and
  exaggerated combat poses with enough pixels for face, clothing, and material cues.
- **Do not inherit:** Side-scroller staging, its red/blue neon scheme, exact costumes,
  or anime-action anatomy wholesale.
- **Verdict:** **KEEP — vendorable under the page terms.** Closest new open bridge
  between Warped City's detail and comic-book energy.

### 2. Gothicvania Patreon's Collection

- **Creator:** ansimuz
- **Source:** [OpenGameArt submission](https://opengameart.org/content/gothicvania-patreons-collection)
- **Direct preview:** [official OGA preview](https://opengameart.org/sites/default/files/styles/medium/public/cover_25.png)
- **License:** CC0 1.0. Exact source: the submission's **License(s): CC0** field and
  [CC0 legal tool](https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/).
- **What to learn:** The nearest open-license sibling to Warped City: dark outer
  contours, economical cel-like clusters, readable humanoid and monster silhouettes,
  and animation that retains character at modest resolution.
- **Do not inherit:** Gothic costumes, Castlevania staging, uniformly gloomy hues, or
  its smaller environmental tile scale.
- **Verdict:** **KEEP — vendorable.** Strongest open sprite-style bridge from Warped
  City toward technofantasy.

### 3. Cyberpunk Portraits

- **Creator:** Hyptosis
- **Source:** [OpenGameArt submission](https://opengameart.org/content/cyberpunk-portraits)
- **Direct preview:** [official OGA preview](https://opengameart.org/sites/default/files/styles/medium/public/cyber_faces_002.png)
- **License:** CC0 1.0. Exact source: the submission's **License(s): CC0** field and
  [CC0 legal tool](https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/).
- **What to learn:** Saturday-morning-cartoon faces, decisive contour breaks, large
  shadow shapes, expressive casting, and a natural visual bridge between sprites,
  dialogue/UI portraits, and comic panels.
- **Do not inherit:** Advance-Wars-like framing, portrait-only proportions, or the
  individual costumes as faction canon.
- **Verdict:** **KEEP — vendorable.** Excellent reference for making the UI's comic
  language feel native to the characters.

### 4. 3 Cyberpunk Characters

- **Creator:** CraftPix.net 2D Game Assets
- **Source:** [OpenGameArt submission](https://opengameart.org/content/3-cyberpunk-characters)

- **License:** OGA-BY 3.0. Exact source: the submission's **License(s): OGA-BY 3.0**

## Tier 2: high-quality official references — link-only

Every item below has **no permissive reuse license stated on its official source**.
Treat the art as copyrighted and **LINK-ONLY**: retain these URLs, but do not commit
copies or derivative crops.

### 7. Huntdown

- **Creator:** Easy Trigger Games
- **Source:** [official Huntdown site](https://huntdown.com/)
- **Direct official preview:** [official screenshot](https://huntdown.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Huntdown_Screenshot1.jpg)
- **License/source:** No permissive art license stated; the official page identifies
  Huntdown as an Easy Trigger Games trademark. Link-only.
- **What to learn:** The best whole-direction reference here: hand-painted 16-bit
  pixels, hard-boiled comic caricature, gang silhouettes, chunky readable weapons,
  neon/grime environments, and character animation that stays sharp at speed.
- **Do not inherit:** 1980s parody wholesale, side-scroller composition, constant
  neon, or its specific bounty-hunter/gang iconography.
- **Verdict:** **KEEP — LINK-ONLY, primary.** Closest external match to the proposed
  comic-book cyberpunk pixel style.

### 8. MARVEL Cosmic Invasion

- **Creator:** Tribute Games; published by Dotemu with Marvel
- **Source:** [official Dotemu press kit](https://www.dotemu.com/PressKit/project/Games/MarvelCosmicInvasion)

- **License/source:** Official press kit states `© 2026 MARVEL. Game Software © 2026

- **What to learn:** This is the clearest pixel/comic marriage: ink-minded contour

- **Do not inherit:** Marvel anatomy, costumes, cosmic-primary palette, or constant

## Filtered-out directions

- Do not use 16x16 roguelike/icon packs as character-style references. They answer a
  different readability problem and pull away from the preferred detail level.
- Do not use vector or pre-rendered sprites merely because they have many animation
  frames. Their smoothing, tweening, and material language do not test the proposed
  comic-book pixel hypothesis.
- Do not treat “cyberpunk” as sufficient. Generic neon stock packs without strong
  pose acting, contour decisions, or character-local palettes add subject matter but
  no useful art-direction signal.

## Suggested first comparison set

For the next art experiment, show the artist and critic the same six references:

1. Warped City animation sheet (existing board baseline)
2. Gothicvania Patreon's Collection (open, rendering bridge)
3. Cyberpunk Portraits (open, comic/UI bridge)
4. Huntdown (link-only, cyberpunk/comic whole-direction target)
5. MARVEL Cosmic Invasion (link-only, powers and comic pose target)
6. ANNO: Mutationem (link-only, detail-density target)

This set is intentionally small enough to communicate a direction. The remaining
references are a library to swap in when a brief specifically tests animation,
cyberarmor, technofantasy, palette, or cinematic lighting.

## Addendum: Kingdom Rush Alliance and Dwarf Fortress Premium

These are useful together because they share **graphic, iconic character design**,
not because they use the same production format.

### Kingdom Rush 5: Alliance — illustrated cartoon sprites

- **Official sources:** [Ironhide game page](https://www.ironhidegames.com/Games/kingdom-rush-alliance),
  [official Steam page](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2849080/Kingdom_Rush_5_Alliance_TD/),
  and Ironhide's [series press kit](https://play.kingdomrush.com/kingdom-rush-presskit).
- **Direct official previews:** [Ironhide screenshot 1](https://files.ironhidegames.com/ironhidegames-www-upfiles/original/Games/deabc280-d017-4333-91d3-2402eeb5cdd5.jpg)
  and [screenshot 2](https://files.ironhidegames.com/ironhidegames-www-upfiles/original/Games/1a898eee-8045-4d70-8121-397775d4ecb1.jpg).
- **What it is actually called:** Ironhide calls the series look “high definition
  awesome illustrated cartoon art” and its battles “cartoon battles.” Alliance's
  official listing calls it 2D, while “Cartoon” and “Cartoony” are user tags rather
  than production documentation. No official source found specifies vector source
  art, raster source art, frame-by-frame animation, a cutout/skeletal rig, or sprite
  dimensions. The safe term is **illustrated cartoon sprites**; the exported JPG and
  AVIF previews do not reveal the authoring pipeline.
- **What to learn:** Heavy silhouette exaggeration, large contour-bound color zones,
  face/equipment cues that survive a distant camera, and character-local palettes.
  The UI can be dark or neutral while characters carry saturated faction colors; a
  global yellow cast is unnecessary.
- **Do not inherit:** Chibi medieval proportions, rubbery comedy in every pose, or
  the assumption that visible segmented motion proves skeletal animation.
- **Rights:** Ironhide states all rights reserved, and its press-kit material is for
  press use. **LINK-ONLY.**

### Dwarf Fortress Premium — layered static pixel sprites

- **Official sources:** [Dwarf Fortress on Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/975370/Dwarf_Fortress/)
  and the [Bay 12 development log](https://www.bay12games.com/dwarves/).
- **Direct official previews:** [fortress and UI screenshot](https://shared.fastly.steamstatic.com/store_item_assets/steam/apps/975370/extras/a3ad21ef2d0dfce3e44247e19dbd6edf.avif?t=1779740424)
  and [creature/UI screenshot](https://shared.fastly.steamstatic.com/store_item_assets/steam/apps/975370/extras/6e7939d313a86446bbd44cc34dc553d8.avif?t=1779740424).
- **What it is actually called:** The official listing says “pixel graphics,” an
  “all-new sprite pack,” and “static 2D sprites.” Bay 12 documents **layer sets** for
  dwarf and creature graphics, with palette layers linked to equipment, material,
  and body appearance. It also notes that dwarves can exceed 32 pixels in height and
  creatures can reach 64 pixels. This supports **layered, palette-driven pixel
  sprites on a roughly 32-pixel map cell**, not cutout animation. It does not mean
  every GUI panel is pixel art: the current interface combines conventional panels
  and text with pixel tiles, icons, and full-body character images.
- **What to learn:** Identity can come from a small number of composable layers—hair,
  armor, held item, body feature, and material palette—without reducing everyone to
  a tiny generic icon. Let silhouettes cross the nominal cell when hair, armor, or a
  rare creature needs it.
- **Do not inherit:** Information density, low-detail map scale, uniformly square
  framing, or static presentation for Hazard Pay's showcase combatants.
- **Rights:** No permissive license for the Premium sprite pack is stated on the
  official sources. **LINK-ONLY.** Moddability does not make the bundled art open.

### Shared language for Hazard Pay

Use **comic-book pixel art** as the art-direction term. It describes the intended
marriage without asserting an unverified toolchain: Kingdom Rush contributes the
cartoon/comic grammar; Dwarf Fortress contributes pixel discipline and systemic
layering. In a production brief, be more exact:

- **Rendering:** medium-resolution pixel sprites with deliberate clusters, not
  low-resolution icon art.
- **Design:** bold outer contours, exaggerated silhouettes, and a few large interior
  value/color zones.
- **System:** optional layered equipment, hair, armor, and palette variants; bespoke
  art remains available for rare espers and special cyberarmor.
- **Color:** character-local palettes over a mostly neutral UI, with yellow reduced
  to one optional accent rather than the ambient color of the whole product.

Avoid **vector sprites**, **skeletal sprites**, **cutout animation**, or **hand-drawn
frame-by-frame** as umbrella terms until Hazard Pay chooses and tests an actual
authoring pipeline.
