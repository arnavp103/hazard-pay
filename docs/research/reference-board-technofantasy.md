# Technofantasy character, power, and cyberarmor references

Research date: 2026-07-22

## Recommendation

Treat technofantasy as an **exploration lane**, not a fixed explanation of the
world. The useful shared grammar is visual:

- Espers can read as genuinely supernatural or remain deliberately ambiguous;
  their effects should feel authored, rare, and personally distinctive.
- Cyberarmor should read as scarce investment through tailored silhouettes,
  layered materials, bespoke deployment animation, and immaculate effects—not
  improvised or cheap hardware.
- Comic-book force should come from anticipation, held key poses, silhouette-breaking
  arcs, impact frames, and selective graphic shapes. It does not require abandoning
  pixel clusters for smooth illustration.
- Color should be swappable by character, faction, power, or experiment. Yellow and
  chartreuse are options, not the global definition of energy or technology.

The strongest compact reference set is **Read Only Memories: NEURODIVER** for an
explicitly invested-in esper, **Star Renegades** for cyberarmor and large power
effects, **Narita Boy** for mythic technology, **SANABI** for comic-book motion,
and **Hyper Light Drifter** for unexplained power and palette restraint. The open
assets are useful production ingredients, but none should define the final style by
itself.

## Rights boundary

Two tiers are intentionally separated:

1. **Open-license candidates:** files may be vendored only with the title, creator,
   source, and license metadata preserved. Re-check the source page immediately
   before copying any file.
2. **Official references:** copyrighted work for discussion only. Keep the official
   links; do not download, crop, mirror, or commit their images.

## Tier 1: open-license candidates safe to vendor

### 1. Warped Mecha Boss

- **Creator:** ansimuz (Luis Zuno)
- **Source:** [creator's itch.io page](https://ansimuz.itch.io/warped-mecha-boss)
- **Direct stable preview:** [creator-hosted boss preview](https://img.itch.zone/aW1hZ2UvNDc1MTcwLzI0Mzk0OTIucG5n/347x500/fSQvuG.png)
- **License/rights source:** The creator page permits personal and commercial use,
  modification, and distribution inside a project without required credit; preserve
  its stated no-generative-AI restriction.
- **What to learn:** Large readable armor plates, modular construction, visible
  damage states, and a machinery silhouette that feels singular rather than
  mass-issued. It is already compatible with the Warped City baseline.
- **What not to inherit:** Boss scale, literal bipedal-mecha anatomy, Contra staging,
  or its exact weapon arrangement.
- **Verdict:** **KEEP — vendorable specialist reference.** Best open starting point
  for bespoke, high-investment cyberarmor.

### 2. Super Clone Cyborg

- **Creator:** Emcee Flesher
- **Source:** [creator submission on OpenGameArt](https://opengameart.org/node/101322)
- **Direct stable preview:** [official submission preview](https://opengameart.org/sites/default/files/styles/medium/public/60001.png)
- **License/rights source:** The creator identifies the model as original and the
  submission releases it under
  [CC0 1.0](https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/).
- **What to learn:** An unusually exact cyberarmor/technomagic combination: eight
  directions, extensive casting/melee/ranged pose coverage, a gunblade, cortical
  module, claw gauntlet, energy projector, and materializer.
- **What not to inherit:** Literal equipment names, top-down RPG staging, identical
  armor on every unit, or the full 256-pose scope as a minimum requirement.
- **Verdict:** **KEEP — vendorable primary cyberarmor prototype.** It can cheaply test
  whether bespoke gear and impossible effects share a readable silhouette.

### 3. Gothicvania Magic Pack 9

- **Creator:** ansimuz (Luis Zuno)
- **Source:** [creator submission on OpenGameArt](https://opengameart.org/content/gothicvania-magic-pack-9)

## Filtered-out patterns

- **16x16 icons and roguelike sprites:** reject as character or armor references.
  They cannot show tailored materials, expressive anatomy, or invested-in equipment.
- **Generic neon cyberpunk packs:** reject when the only contribution is cyan,
  magenta, or chartreuse subject matter. A reference must teach shape, motion,
  material, or color hierarchy.
- **Smooth vector/tweened packs:** reject as pixel-style evidence even when their
  animation lists are extensive. They can inform timing, but not cluster language.
- **Literal medieval fantasy:** reject as a default. A magic reference stays useful
  only when its effect, pose, or value structure can be separated from its costume.
- **One universal “esper look”:** reject. The art system should support multiple
  explanations and multiple palette/shape families rather than turning experimentation
  into a lore contradiction.
- **REPLACED as a character target:** reject despite its excellent pixel lighting.
  Its realistic proportions, cinematic 2.5D, and restrained acting move away from the
  cartoony comic energy the interface invites.
- **32x32 cyborg and magic packs:** reject from the visual board. Their action lists
  can be useful production checklists, but they repeat the low-pixel problem and do
  not communicate rare investment.

## Suggested first experiment

Build one throwaway comparison using the same rare unit in three treatments:

1. **NEURODIVER lane:** clean institutional presentation, expressive portrait, and a
   psychic effect whose source remains natural or supernatural.
2. **Star Renegades lane:** tailored cyberarmor, large silhouette-breaking effect,
   and premium material highlights.
3. **Narita Boy / Hyper Light Drifter lane:** mythic, unexplained geometry with a
   restricted non-yellow palette.

Phrase the same attack with **SANABI**-style anticipation and impact. This isolates
the decisions the board actually needs to support—density, comic force, armor
investment, effect language, and palette—without deciding what espers ultimately are.
