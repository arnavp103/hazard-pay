# Environment references for the comic-book cyberpunk direction

## Question and filter

Which external environment references can help Hazard Pay combine the readable,
expressive pixel density of **Warped City** with comic-book shape language,
layered cyberpunk spaces, and palettes that do not default to acid chartreuse?

This survey deliberately separates work that may be copied into a public repo
from copyrighted work that must stay link-only. A `Keep` verdict means the
reference contributes a distinct lesson to the board; it does **not** mean its
whole style or palette should be inherited.

## Recommended cut

The strongest compact set is:

1. **REPLACED** for cinematic depth, weather, and value staging.
2. **Huntdown** for the clearest marriage of comic attitude and detailed pixel
   environments.
3. **The Last Night** for dense street layers and low-fi/high-fi rendering.
4. **Eastward** for colorful, illustrated pixel environments that stay readable.
5. **Cyberpunk Street Environment** for an open-license street scene with grime
   and parallax depth.
6. **Post Apocalyptic Pixel Art Backgrounds** for warm rust/teal and dusty,
   non-chartreuse industrial palettes.
7. **Pixel Art Street Backgrounds** for market/storefront clutter and neighborhood
   specificity.
8. **Warped City 2** for a safely reproducible density and silhouette companion
   to the existing Warped City anchor.

## Synthesis for Hazard Pay

The references converge on a flexible system rather than a fixed palette:

- Build scenes from **three to five large value/contour masses first**, then add
  pixel grime, signs, pipes, market goods, and windows selectively.
- Treat the comic-book quality as **shape and staging**—thick readable silhouettes,
  graphic light pools, localized exaggeration—not as smooth vector outlines laid
  over pixel art.
- Let each district own a limited accent family: rust/teal, coral/violet,
  red/cyan, sodium orange/slate, or low-saturation grey-purple. Chartreuse can
  remain one option, not the city-wide default.
- Preserve tactical readability by putting the richest texture in near and far
  environmental bands while keeping the unit plane quieter.
- Use modern effects such as haze, bloom, reflections, and parallax only to
  clarify depth after the pixel/value composition already reads.

The key stylistic marriage is therefore **Warped City/Eastward pixel density +
Huntdown comic attitude + REPLACED/The Last Night depth discipline**, with palette
families swapped per experiment or district.
